Tragic End

December 2017 admin  

Just like my other blogs, we will start off with the definition of landslides. Landslides as we all know is the disaster that occurs when the soil in the mountains soften because of the interference of a third party phenomenon. Landslides are defined as mass movement of rock, debris or earth down a slope and have come to include broad range of motions whereby falling, sliding and flowing under the influence of gravity dislodges earth material.

This chapter also covers the types of landslides.

Plant a tree to prevent a mountain's pee

Do you know what causes landslides? The causes of landslides can be distinguished into two. First is the natural factors like the gravity, geological factors, heavy and prolonged rainfall, earthquakes, forest fires, volcanoes, waves, and freezing and thawing .The other cause of landslides are the anthropogenic factors, these are the inappropriate drainage system, cutting and deep excavations on slopes for buildings, roads, canals, and mining, included also is the change in slope or land use pattern, deforestation, settlements, agricultural practices on steep slopes

If there’s a cause, then there’ll be surely an effect. What are these effects you say? Well, landslides destroys everything and anything that comes in their path including rail lines, lines of communication, settlements and roads. Take note that fatalities depends on the place and time of occurrence. If you would look up to records, the estimated casualties per year, world-wide would be 600 to 1000 persons.

How would I know if there is a possibility of landslide in our area?

These signs would help you so that you would be aware and at the same time be prepared in advance. So make sure that you memorize or atleast familiarize all the tips that I will insert here

Springs, seeps, or saturated ground in areas that have not typically been wet before.

New cracks or unusual bulges in the ground, street pavements or sidewalks.

Soil moving away from foundations, and ancillary structures such as decks and patios tilting and/or moving relative to the house

Sticking doors and windows, and visible open spaces.

Broken water lines and other underground utilities.

Leaning telephone poles, trees, retaining walls or fences.

Sunken or dropped-down road beds.

Sudden decrease in creek water levels even though rain is still falling or just recently stopped.

After a landslide, keep yourself safe and secured and if possible you should also check for injured and trapped persons, without entering the slide and direct and assist rescuers. If you have portable ones, listen to local radio or television station. Watch for flooding, which may occur after a landslide or debris flow.

Sinkhole was always there. It was just waiting for someone to trigger it.

Okay, what are sinkholes? A sinkhole is a hole in the ground that forms when water dissolves surface rock. This happens when a landscape, where carbonate rock sits underneath the soil, is exposed to water. There are actually just two types of sink holes; cover-collapsed sinkholes- which develop quickly in a matter of hours and cause catastrophic damage. And the other one, cover-subsidence sinkholes- which form slowly over time with the ground gradually subsiding or deflating.

How to avoid sinkholes?

  • Treating underground limestone.
  • Sea water treatment facilities.
  • Recycling gray water run-offs.
  • Avoid construction on wetlands.
